Cebu

Cebu has been described as the land that time forgot. It is one of the last refuges of the ogres. It is a primal land, filled with mega-fauna and hunter-gatherers.

History

Cebu has long been isolated from mainland Trystell, both culturally and geographically. Because of this distance it is something of a throwback—a land that time forgot. Not only is it the last refuge of the ogres, but it is also home to an impressive array of mega-fauna.

Sometime in the centuries after the War of the Shattering the ancestors of the island's ogres established their tribal culture. Here they found the island uninhabited, and started to rebuild their culture as they saw fit. They fashioned a culture centered around the tribe, where magic was taboo and where status came with hunting the great game of the island. In later times the island would be subject to several gnomish migrations and a later crusade by Grichom to drive heretics from, the region. These latter invaders built on the island a colony with a working port. This one city on the island, however, has long since fallen under the shadow of the surrounding gnomish tribes.

Culture

Most of Cebu still retains a hunter-gatherer culture, barely in the bronze age. Tribal loyalties on the island run strong, and the native ogres, gnomes and humans/dragonborn tend to stick with their own.

Magic has a particularly strong taboo among the tribes on the island, and among some tribes “witches” are killed upon discovery, their heads left on pikes as a warning to others. Cebu is also one of the few regions on Trystell where the Common language hasn't penetrated far from Lalog colony.

Major Region Features

  • Gimbi Forest: Gimbi Forest is made up mostly of an extremely large species of elm trees that only grow on Cebu Island. This species of trees is known for its high-quality, waterresistant wood and is often sought in ship construction by those who have large sums of money. The locals make sure not to cut down too many trees. If they did, it would not only destroy the trees, but it would drive down prices.
  • Gimloo: Gimloo is a tribal camp whose economy is centered around extraction of natural resources. Logging goes on in the south, mining in the west. These resources are then typically shipped downriver to Lalog where they make their way by port to the far corners of Trystell.
  • Lagden Mountains: The Lagden Mountains are a small mountain chain on Cebu. This chain is rich in a variety of different ores and home to many different dangerous, but valuable, species whose hides fetch a sizable price elsewhere. The peaks are capped in glaciers.
  • Lagden River: The Lagden River is a surprisingly sizable river, despite being on an island. It is fed by the glaciers that sit high in the Lagden Mountains and is it is said that its water possesses restorative properties.
  • Lalog: The lone settled colony on Cebu, Lalog was founded during a Grichom-funded crusade to the island a generation ago. Today, however, the city is under the rule of the much-feared gnomish Wrathwrench clan. They demand tribute from the city and take a cut of all goods being shipped through the port..
  • Nirifte: This large encampment exists mostly as a trading grounds for hunters and trappers, although it seasonally becomes the nexus and meeting point for the various tribes found across western Cebu. It also serves as the largest fishing encampment on the island, and during the appropriate season boats will regularly travel between here and Lalog.
  • Ruby Mountain: Ruby Mountain is one of the fabled gem mountains. It is a sacred place to the followers of Zuraic and guarded by a strange lizard creature. However, there have been strange rumors of this mountain slowly crumbling; something not occurring on the other gem mountains.
